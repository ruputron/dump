Commodore C=64.

As you might have already noticed I'm a fan of the Commodore 64, a classic home computer from the 1980s that still has a strong following and userbase today. New games and software, music for the awesome 6581 and 8580 SID sound chips and demos that are made to really test the limitations of the aging C64 hardware.

CSDB.

Recent years have been interesting in the Commodore scene, I'm pretty new to it all really (apart from having a C64 Breadbin when I was at high school) but this stuff is really fascinating to me, people who know what they're doing with their programming skills making awesome stuff for the old C64, demoscene peeps doing stuff that's really impressive considering the tech limitations (C64 and a lot of old systems, probably all of them). Here's a linky if you, for whatever reason don't already know about this awesome site. CSDB \o/

Contrast In Games.

I feel like it really adds a lot when a game has a good feeling of atmosphere and it's nice to break up the more intense parts of gameplay with some chill time. Resident Evil is probably the first example of this kind of thing I can think of. When games began using CD quality audio it was a big deal, it really made things feel more believeable and gave you a very different experience than the older sound chips we were used to at the time.

Modern(ish) Tech.

I appreciate what some game devs and musicians have done with more modern tech. I often like to sit and relax in some of the more quiet areas of games these days listening to the ambient music or sounds. This kind of thing can massively improve my gaming experience and opinion of a game.
